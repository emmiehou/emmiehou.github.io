kitchen cart

a versatile kitchen cart combining industrial metal construction iwth a handcrafted butcher block top, designed for everyday functionality and style in industrial kitchens. this was my final project for a junior-year metalshop class, where we had a $120 material budget and approxmiately 24 hours of in-class build time over 19 days.

design process

here's a breakdown of what i did:

  • created detailed plans using Rhino for precise measurements and visual layout
  • built frame using 3/4” square metal tubing, carefully welded for strength and stability
  • cut metal shelves from sheet metal using a cnc laser cutter based on custom rhino designs, then welded into place
  • crafted the butcher block top from carefully selected hardwood strips—glued, planed, sanded, and finished with beeswax oil
  • applied a clear-coat metal protectant to preserve the steel finish
  • installed caster wheels for mobility
  • added a handle for easy transport, hooks for practical storage
  • securely mounted the butcher block top to complete the piece

materials

  • 3/4" square metal pipe
  • sheet metal
  • butcher block top (scrapwood)
  • heavy-duty locking caster wheels
  • clear-coated metal protectant

features

  • mobile design with locking wheels
  • durable butcher block work surface
  • handle for practical maneuvering
  • hooks for hanging items
  • industrial-grade construction
