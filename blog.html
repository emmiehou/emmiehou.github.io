First perfusion!!!! What?!!

December 9, 2025

This is a photo of me extracting the brain in my first perfusion surgery!!! A perfusion is where we euthanize a mouse after it's been run in our experiements, and then run PFA through the heart to clear the body of blood, and then extract the brain to do anatomical analysis on regions of interest. Big step for me to learn how to perform this surgery. Definitely a bit queesy from the blood and guts, but I'm really proud of myself for learning and actually doing well! My fellow intern, Joey, does most of the perfusions for our PI, but she taught me super patiently and I managed to end up extracting 3 brains!

Biobserve data analysis

August 4, 2025

I started analyzing maze data using Biobserve zones to detect frequency of the different paths the mice took. This was a big first step in the strategical analysis of the experiement in trying to quantify decision-making behaviors. Our goal is to track how many trips mice took in each of the 3 possible paths to get to the reward pellet at the end of the maze. In the future, with live location tracking, we hope to stimulate specific regions of the brain to see if that alters their decision making at important intersections in the maze.

Slicing mice brains

June 27, 2025

I've sliced many many mouse brains using the vibratome and stained them with fluorescent proteins to visualize the mPFC neural circuits. Here's a few regions I sliced and mounted today. I enjoy slicing on the vibratome because I find it somewhat therapeutic; it's a nice repetitive task that I find calming, and the slices are so thin and delicate. It's beautiful to look at and see the anatomy of such a tiny brain. Sometimes when I'm slicing I try to imagine what our human brains would look like if we sliced them like I slice the mouse brains.

Improved RuleDuino

June 2025

Reverse engineered and improved the "RuleDuino." This device is used widely in our lab to record live data and laser stimulate target regions of the brain during behavioral experiments. After figuring out the schematic, I designed the most optimal layout within the constraints of the cardboard box being the vessel, and created a "RuleDuino" with a more stable structure with a cleaner appearance. I think it looks really nice and makes it more efficient to run animals through experiments and keep all the numbers organized.
